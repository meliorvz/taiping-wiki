# Local Memory and Genealogies

Lineage genealogies, 族谱, zúpǔ, and local memory texts help recover the social history of the Taiping war.[^meyer] They record deaths, migrations, adoptions, widows, property loss, rebuilding, and claims of loyalty.

## What genealogies can show

A genealogy may show branches that disappeared, households that moved, sons adopted to continue a line, women praised for chastity or survival, and ancestors reburied after the war. These details help historians see the war below the level of capitals and commanders.

## What genealogies hide

Genealogies are produced by surviving lineages. They may silence shame, collaboration, poverty, illegitimacy, flight, or conflict inside the family. They often reflect male elite priorities. Read them with gazetteers, inscriptions, tax records, and diaries.

## Why memory mattered

After a civil war, communities had to decide what the dead meant. Were they loyal? Were they victims? Did they die because of rebels, Qing troops, hunger, or disorder? Memory helped rebuild authority.
