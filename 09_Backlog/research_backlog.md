## Progress in this integration session (5 June 2026)

### What was accomplished

- **18 sources fully integrated** into the wiki's working corpus: Michael (3 vols), Luo Ergang 太平天国史 (4 vols), Luo Ergang 全集 (22 vols), Mao Jiaqi 太平天国通史 (3 vols), Liu Chen 太平天国再研究 (2025), Kuhn, Reilly, Meyer-Fong, Lindley (2 vols), Wilson Ever-Victorious Army, Li Xiucheng critical edition, Ying Jie Gui Zhen facsimile, British Parliamentary Papers Vol 32, 太平天国史料 (1955), 太平天国资料 (2013), and the North-China Herald collection (~3900 issues).
- **Core Taiping texts collated** against Michael vols II-III: 天朝田亩制度, 资政新篇, 原道救世歌, and 原道醒世训 cross-checked between Wikisource and published translations.
- **Source base surveys completed**: a previously Wikipedia-reliant wiki now has primary scholarly editions from Chinese, English, and treaty-port sources.
- **Backlog restructured**: new tracking documents created for source gaps, dangerous claims, and next-page priorities.
- **Chronology expanded**: missing dates added for Hong Xiuquan's exam failures, Feng Yunshan's Guangxi organizing (1844-1847), Shi Dakai's departure (1857), Sanhe victory (1858), Anqing campaign (1860-1861), Suzhou fall (1863), and other key turning points.
- **Major leaders table updated**: added Xiao Chaogui, Lin Fengxiang, Li Kaifang, Zuo Zongtang, Sengge Rinchen, and cross-references among late-war figures.

### Remaining gaps

- **Primary documents**: the 26-volume 清政府镇压太平天国档案史料 remains the single largest gap. Without it, the wiki's Qing-side coverage relies on secondary scholarship and British treaty-port sources, missing the internal Qing court perspective.
- **Local records**: 6 of 9 provincial gazetteer sets still missing. County-level social history, economic disruption, and post-war reconstruction claims remain thin or reliant on Meyer-Fong's single-province Jiangnan analysis.
- **Foreign diplomacy**: French and US diplomatic files not yet searched. The wiki cannot fully substantiate claims about Great Power policy, neutrality enforcement, or arms supply networks.
- **Maps and visual material**: no start. Spatial analysis of campaigns, city layouts, and regional devastation is not yet possible.
- **North-China Herald**: acquired but not indexed. 3900 issues require systematic keyword search before claims sourced to "contemporary foreign press" can be verified.
- **Calendar conversion**: not started. Chronology entries currently lack Taiping-calendar equivalents, hindering precise cross-referencing with primary documents dated in the Taiping system.
- **Non-Western, non-PRC scholarship**: Japanese, Taiwanese, and Hong Kong bibliography not yet compiled. The wiki's historiography leans heavily on PRC and English-language perspectives.
