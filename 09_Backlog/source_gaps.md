# Source Gaps

*Created 5 June 2026. Documents known gaps in the wiki's source base and what each gap means for the reliability of current claims.*

---

## 1. Missing primary sources

### 清政府镇压太平天国档案史料 (26 vols)

**What it is:** The Qing central government's internal archival record of the entire Taiping war — memorials, edicts, intelligence reports, interrogation records, and military correspondence compiled from the Grand Council and Grand Secretariat archives by 中国第一历史档案馆.

**What it would enable:** Verifying or correcting every claim about Qing decision-making, troop movements, logistics, casualty reporting, and provincial coordination. Currently the wiki relies on secondary scholarship (Luo Ergang, Mao Jiaqi) and British treaty-port observations for the Qing side.

**Status:** Not found. May exist via CNKI, 读秀, or Baidu Wangpan; likely behind institutional paywalls. Physical copies held by major research libraries.

**Consequence for current claims:** Any statement about Qing strategy, provincial coordination, or internal court politics is derived from secondary interpretation rather than archival verification.

---

### French diplomatic files (Archives du MAE, série Chine)

**What it is:** French consular and diplomatic correspondence from Shanghai, Ningbo, and Beijing during the Taiping period. Covers Catholic missionary relations with the Taiping, French neutrality policy, and assessments of Taiping governance.

**What it would enable:** Rounding out the treaty-port perspective beyond British sources. The French maintained a distinct position on Catholicism in Taiping theology that British Protestant observers often misrepresented or ignored. Would also illuminate the French role in the Ningbo and Shanghai fighting.

**Status:** Not found. Not yet searched at the French diplomatic archives' digital portals.

**Consequence for current claims:** The wiki's "foreign involvement" narrative is heavily British-weighted. French policy, missionary-Taiping interactions, and the Catholic dimension of Taiping Christianity are undertreated.

---

### US diplomatic files (FRUS 1860-1864)

**What it is:** *Foreign Relations of the United States* volumes covering the American Civil War era, including correspondence from US consuls in Shanghai regarding the Taiping, the Ward/Burgevine affair, and US neutrality enforcement.

**What it would enable:** Adding the American perspective on Ward's filibustering, US arms sales to both sides, and American missionary assessments of the Taiping. Fills the third major Western power's view.

**Status:** Not searched. Known to be available via history.state.gov and the University of Wisconsin digital collections.

**Consequence for current claims:** The Ward/Burgevine story is currently told through British (Wilson) and Chinese secondary sources. Missing the US diplomatic and legal record.

---

### 《太平天国印书》 complete facsimile edition

**What it is:** The definitive facsimile collection of all Taiping printed books, edicts, and proclamations. Contains the original woodblock-printed versions of the complete Taiping corpus.

**What it would enable:** Verifying the exact wording and appearance of Taiping publications, resolving textual variants between editions, and providing visual sources for the wiki.

**Status:** Partial. Some items appear in 民国丛书 on Internet Archive, but not the complete facsimile set.

**Consequence for current claims:** Core text citations currently rely on Wikisource (digital transcriptions) and Michael (English translations). Neither fully substitutes for the original woodblock editions for philological precision.

---

### 《太平天国史料丛编》

**What it is:** A multi-volume compilation of Taiping-period primary documents assembled by PRC historians, including local notices, land deeds, tax receipts, and other non-canonical materials not in the central archives.

**What it would enable:** Reconstructing local administration, economic practices, and social life under Taiping rule beyond the central texts.

**Status:** Not found.

**Consequence for current claims:** Claims about Taiping local governance, tax collection, and daily administration are based on secondary scholarship rather than primary documentary evidence from Taiping-held territories.

## 2. Missing local records

### Provincial gazetteers (地方志) — 6 provinces missing entirely

| Province | Status | Significance for wiki |
|----------|--------|-----------------------|
| Guangxi | MISSING | Origins of the rebellion; Jintian, Yong'an, Guiping local context |
| Hunan | MISSING | Xiang Army recruitment base; Xiao Chaogui's early area; Changsha siege |
| Hubei | MISSING | Wuchang occupation; middle Yangzi campaigns |
| Jiangsu | MISSING | Tianjing/Nanjing vicinity; Jiangnan warfare core zone |
| Zhejiang | MISSING | Li Xiucheng's eastern campaigns; Ward's death at Cixi |
| Sichuan | MISSING | Shi Dakai's final campaign; Dadu River crossing |
| Anhui | PARTIAL | Anqing region; northern Yangzi warfare |
| Jiangxi | PARTIAL | Shi Dakai's campaigns; Taiping administrative experimentation |
| Fujian | PARTIAL | Remnant Taiping forces 1864-1866 |

**What complete gazetteer coverage would enable:**
- County-level demographic change estimates
- Verification of local destruction and reconstruction claims
- Identification of local elite responses (militia formation, collaboration, resistance)
- Ground-truthing campaign routes and battle locations
- Documenting post-war commemorative practices (temples, stelae, local histories)

**Consequence for current claims:** The wiki makes general statements about "devastation in Jiangnan" but lacks the granularity to distinguish between counties, track population recovery, or compare regional experiences. Meyer-Fong's *What Remains* provides the best available framework but focuses on Jiangnan; the Yangzi upriver and southern provinces have no equivalent treatment in the wiki's source base.

## 3. Missing scholarship

### Japanese-language scholarship

**Why it matters:** Japanese historians have produced extensive Taiping scholarship since the early 20th century, particularly on social structure, religious origins, and comparative rebellion studies. Key figures include Ichiko Chūzō (市古宙三), Kojima Shinji (小島晋治), and Namiki Yorihisa (並木頼寿). The Japanese tradition is especially strong on the Hakka dimension and grassroots organization.

**Status:** Not started. No Japanese sources in the wiki's bibliography.

**Consequence for current claims:** The wiki's discussion of Hakka identity, social structure, and religious organization may be missing key arguments from the Japanese historiographical tradition.

### Taiwan and Hong Kong scholarship

**Why it matters:** Taiwan-based historians (following Jian Youwen 简又文) produced independent scholarship during the Cold War that diverged from PRC Marxist interpretations. Hong Kong scholars working from British colonial archives have unique access to treaty-port materials. Key figures: Jian Youwen, Wang Ermin (王爾敏), Kwang-Ching Liu (劉廣京).

**Status:** Not started.

**Consequence for current claims:** The wiki's historiographical framing follows PRC periodization. Alternative interpretations — particularly on religion, foreign relations, and the post-war legacy — are underrepresented.

### Post-2000 Western scholarship

| Work | Status | Why needed |
|------|--------|------------|
| Jonathan Spence, *God's Chinese Son* (1996) | NOT ACQUIRED | Most widely read English-language biography of Hong Xiuquan; shapes popular understanding |
| Carl Kilcourse, *Taiping Theology* (2016) | NOT ACQUIRED | Major recent treatment of Taiping religious thought |
| Stephen Platt, *Autumn in the Heavenly Kingdom* (2012) | NOT ACQUIRED | Narrative history of the war's final phase; widely cited |
| Chuck Wooldridge, *City of Virtues* (2015) | NOT ACQUIRED | Nanjing urban history during the Qing, including Taiping period |
| Jenne, *The Taiping Rebellion and the British Empire* (dissertation/monograph) | NOT SEARCHED | British imperial context |

**Consequence for current claims:** The wiki's scholarly basis is largely pre-2000 (Michael, Luo Ergang) plus the 2004-2015 wave (Kuhn, Reilly, Meyer-Fong). Post-2015 Anglophone scholarship is absent.

## 4. Missing maps and visual material

### 《太平天国历史地图集》

**What it is:** The standard PRC historical atlas of the Taiping war, with annotated campaign maps, city plans, administrative boundaries, and demographic distribution.

**Status:** Not found.

**Consequence for current claims:** All spatial claims in the wiki — campaign routes, territorial extent, siege locations — lack cartographic verification. The wiki cannot produce original maps.

### Photographic and visual record

- No systematic collection of period visual sources (Felice Beato photographs, Chinese woodblock illustrations, Western newspaper engravings)
- No map of Tianjing city layout in 1853-1864
- No visual documentation of Taiping material culture (coins, seals, uniforms, banners)

**Consequence for current claims:** The wiki is text-only with no visual corroboration for claims about material culture, architecture, or urban geography.

## 5. Missing quantitative data

### Demographic data

- **Pre-war population baselines:** No county-level population figures for the 1840s-1850s in affected provinces.
- **War deaths by county:** Secondary literature offers province-level estimates but not county-level verification.
- **Post-war recovery rates:** No systematic data on population recovery, land recultivation, or tax restoration in the 1870s-1890s.

**Consequence for current claims:** The wiki cannot numerically substantiate its casualty claims or compare regional demographic impact beyond citing secondary literature's aggregate estimates (see 07_Reference/casualty_estimates.md).

### Economic data

- No grain price series for Taiping-affected regions
- No systematic data on land tax collection before, during, or after the war
- No quantitative evidence for the Taiping treasury system (圣库) in practice

**Consequence for current claims:** Claims about the Taiping economic system's implementation and effects remain qualitative and text-based, without quantitative verification.

### Military data

- No systematic troop strength estimates by campaign and date
- No weapon-procurement data (quantities, sources, costs)
- No logistics data (supply routes, depot locations, transport capacity)

**Consequence for current claims:** Tactical and operational claims in campaign pages lack quantitative backing.

---

## 6. Other gaps

### Taiping calendar materials

No primary or secondary source has been acquired that systematically documents the Taiping calendar system (天历). The wiki currently mentions it in passing but cannot explain its structure, divergence from the lunar calendar, or impact on campaign timing.

### Oral history and folklore

No collection of oral traditions, local legends, or folk memories of the Taiping in affected regions. Meyer-Fong's *What Remains* discusses memory practices but the wiki has no direct access to oral or folkloric sources.

### Museum and archival finding aids

- No visit or digital survey of the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom History Museum (南京太平天国历史博物馆) holdings
- No finding aids for provincial or municipal archives in affected regions
- No systematic survey of what the National Palace Museum (Taipei) and other repositories hold
