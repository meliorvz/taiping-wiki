## People pages

### High priority (sources ready)

1. **Feng Yunshan (冯云山)** — The organizer. Michael vol. I covers his Guangxi work 1844-1847; Luo Ergang 太平天国史 vol. 4 has his biography. Page should cover: early life, partnership with Hong, Guangxi organizing, Jintian Uprising role, South King title, death at Quanzhou (May 1852), his underappreciated role as the movement's real builder.
   - *Cross-reference:* Hong Xiuquan, Yang Xiuqing, Origins page, Jintian page (when created)

2. **Yang Xiuqing (杨秀清)** — The strategist and spirit medium. Michael and Luo provide full coverage. Page should cover: charcoal-burner origins, claiming to speak for God, Eastern King role, military command 1851-1856, relationship with Hong, the Tianjing Incident and his death.
   - *Cross-reference:* Hong Xiuquan, Wei Changhui, Tianjing Incident, Western Expeditions

3. **Shi Dakai (石达开)** — The Wing King. Page should cover: early role, military successes, break with Hong after Tianjing Incident (1857), independent campaigns through Jiangxi, Zhejiang, Fujian, Hunan, Guangxi, Guizhou, and Sichuan, capture and execution at Chengdu (1863), the Dadu River tragedy.
   - *Cross-reference:* Tianjing Incident, Campaigns Overview, Sichuan gazetteer (when acquired)

4. **Chen Yucheng (陈玉成)** — The Ying King. Page should cover: rise as young commander, Sanhe victory (1858), Anqing defense, capture and execution by Qing (1862).
   - *Cross-reference:* Li Xiucheng, Anqing Campaign, Sanhe page (when created)

5. **Xiao Chaogui (萧朝贵)** — The Western King. Existing pages mention him but he needs a full biography. Burned charcoal with Yang Xiuqing, also claimed to speak for Jesus, died leading the Changsha assault (September 1852).
   - *Cross-reference:* Yang Xiuqing, Feng Yunshan, Origins page

6. **Lin Fengxiang (林凤翔) and Li Kaifang (李开芳)** — Northern Expedition commanders. Page should cover: their march north 1853-1855, the strategic miscalculation of the expedition, their capture and execution.
   - *Cross-reference:* Northern Expedition page (when created), Campaigns Overview

## Place pages

1. **Guiping / Jintian county page (桂平县/金田村)** — Physical and social geography of the rebellion's birthplace. Requires Guangxi gazetteer (still missing). Content: location, population, ethnic composition (Hakka vs. Guangfu/Punti), local economy, the God Worshippers' early activities, sites of the Jintian Uprising.
   - *Cross-reference:* Origins, Feng Yunshan, Hong Xiuquan, Ethnicity page (when created)

2. **Tianjing / Nanjing urban page (天京/南京)** — The Taiping capital city. Requires maps (still missing) and Jiangsu gazetteer. Content: pre-1853 Nanjing, the capture, urban reorganization under Taiping rule, palace complex, gender-segregated quarters, the siege (1862-1864), the fall, post-war reconstruction.
   - *Cross-reference:* Siege of Tianjing page (when created), Hong Xiuquan, Jiangnan reconstruction page

3. **Yong'an (永安)** — Where the Taiping formed their first court and state structure (September 1851 - April 1852). Content: siege, the 永安建制 (court formation, royal titles), the breakout.
   - *Cross-reference:* Chronology, Feng Yunshan, Yang Xiuqing

4. **Wuchang / Wuhan trilogy (武昌/武汉三镇)** — Captured December 1852, held until February 1853. Content: the assault, occupation, decision to move downriver rather than hold.
   - *Cross-reference:* Chronology, Northern Expedition

5. **Anqing (安庆)** — Strategic middle Yangzi city. Content: early Taiping control, Ye Yulai's defense, the Qing siege (1860-1861), fall in September 1861, its role as gateway to Nanjing.
   - *Cross-reference:* Anqing Campaign page, Chen Yucheng, Zeng Guofan

6. **Suzhou (苏州)** — Major Jiangnan city. Content: fall to Li Xiucheng (1860), Taiping administration, fall to Li Hongzhang (December 1863), the surrender massacre.
   - *Cross-reference:* Li Xiucheng, Li Hongzhang, Suzhou Massacre page (when created)

## Thematic pages

1. **Taiping calendar (天历) conversion guide** — Explain the calendar system: 366-day year, 7-day week with different day names, monthly structure. Provide a conversion table or method for key dates. Sources: Michael vol. II discusses the calendar; PRC reference works contain conversion tables. This is a foundational reference page needed for accurate dating across the wiki.
   - *Cross-reference:* Chronology (every date should have Taiping equivalent)

2. **Taiping Christianity (拜上帝教)** — Synthesize Reilly, the primary texts (原道救世歌, 原道醒世训, 天条书), and the 英杰归真 into a comprehensive page on Taiping theology: God, Jesus, Hong's status, the Ten Commandments adaptation, Sabbath observance, iconoclasm, relationship to Protestant Christianity and Chinese folk religion.
   - *Cross-reference:* Hong Xiuquan, Hong Rengan, Reilly bibliographic entry

3. **The treasury system (圣库制度)** — Separate page on the economic system: theory (all property to the public treasury), practice (varied by time and place), comparison to Chinese utopian precedents (太平经, etc.).
   - *Cross-reference:* 天朝田亩制度, Economic page (if created)

4. **Military system page** — Organization of Taiping armies: the five-army structure (front/rear/left/right/center), unit sizes, officer ranks, women's armies, supply and logistics, weapons.
   - *Cross-reference:* Military Organizations reference page, Campaigns Overview

5. **Women in the Taiping** — Gender policies and practice: women's military units, women's examinations, prohibition of foot-binding, segregation, polygyny, the gap between proclamation and reality.
   - *Cross-reference:* Dangerous Claims #7, Social Policies page

6. **Ethnicity and the Taiping** — Hakka dimension, Punti/Hakka conflict in Guangxi, ethnic composition of leadership, the expansion beyond Hakka areas. Integrate Japanese scholarship when acquired.
   - *Cross-reference:* Origins page, Dangerous Claims #8

7. **Sanhe (三河) victory** — The November 1858 battle where Chen Yucheng and Li Xiucheng annihilated a Xiang Army force under Li Xubin. The Taiping's greatest battlefield victory after the Tianjing Incident and a turning point in the late war.
   - *Cross-reference:* Chen Yucheng, Li Xiucheng, Campaigns Overview

8. **Northern Expedition (北伐) page** — The 1853-1855 march toward Beijing: strategic rationale, route, battles, the miscalculation of sending an unsupported force deep into Qing territory, the commanders' fates.
   - *Cross-reference:* Lin Fengxiang, Li Kaifang, Campaigns Overview

9. **Western Expeditions (西征) page** — The 1853-1856 campaigns to secure the middle Yangzi: Anhui, Jiangxi, Hubei, Hunan. The Xiang Army emerges as the Taiping's most dangerous opponent.
   - *Cross-reference:* Yang Xiuqing, Zeng Guofan, Anqing

10. **Anqing Campaign page** — The 1860-1861 struggle for the strategic gateway to Nanjing. The Qing encirclement strategy, Chen Yucheng's relief attempts, the city's fall, and its consequences.
    - *Cross-reference:* Anqing place page, Chen Yucheng, Zeng Guofan

11. **Suzhou surrender massacre page** — December 1863: the eight Taiping kings who surrendered Suzhou to Li Hongzhang were executed. A controversial episode in Qing conduct of the war.
    - *Cross-reference:* Li Hongzhang, Suzhou place page, Li Xiucheng

12. **PRC historiography page** — Evolution of Taiping scholarship in the People's Republic: the "peasant war" (农民战争) paradigm, Mao's uses of the Taiping, the post-Mao diversification, the 1990s-present re-evaluations. Name key historians (Luo Ergang, Mao Jiaqi, Liu Chen) and their contributions.
    - *Cross-reference:* Liu Chen 2025 bibliographic entry, Dangerous Claims #4
