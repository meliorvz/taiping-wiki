# Chinese-English Glossary

|---|---|---|---|
| 太平天国运动 | Tàipíng Tiānguó yùndòng | Taiping Rebellion or Taiping movement | Full war and movement. |

| 太平军 | Tàipíng jūn | Taiping Army | Armed forces of the movement. |

| 天京 | Tiānjīng | Heavenly Capital | Taiping name for Nanjing. |
| 金田起义 | Jīntián qǐyì | Jintian Uprising | January 1851 beginning of open rebellion. |
| 永安建制 | Yǒng'ān jiànzhì | Yong'an state formation | Early naming of kings and institutions. |
| 东王 | Dōngwáng | Eastern King | Yang Xiuqing's title. |

| 英王 | Yīngwáng | Heroic King | Chen Yucheng's title. |
| 天朝田亩制度 | Tiāncháo tiánmǔ zhìdù | Land System of the Heavenly Dynasty | Taiping land and household program. |
| 资政新篇 | Zīzhèng xīnpiān | New Treatise on Aids to Administration | Hong Rengan's reform proposal. |
| 原道救世歌 | Yuándào jiùshì gē | Song on the Original Way to Save the World | Early Hong religious text. |
| 原道醒世训 | Yuándào xǐngshì xùn | Awakening the World on the Original Way | Early Hong religious text. |
| 原道觉世训 | Yuándào juéshì xùn | Perceiving the World on the Original Way | Early Hong religious text. |

| 礼拜堂 | Lǐbàitáng | Worship hall | Local worship institution. |

| 女馆 | Nǚguǎn | Women's quarters or barracks | Term used for sex separation systems. |
| 男馆 | Nánguǎn | Men's quarters or barracks | Paired male institution. |
| 两司马 | Liǎng sīmǎ | Two sima officers | Local administrative officers in Taiping plan. |
| 天王 | Tiānwáng | Heavenly King | Hong Xiuquan's title. |
| 太平天国历 | Tàipíng Tiānguó lì | Taiping calendar | Taiping calendar system; 366 days, 12 months of 30-31 days, abolished lunar intercalation. |
| 老兄弟 | Lǎo xiōngdì | Old brothers | Taiping veterans who joined before 1853; held higher status than later recruits. |
| 新兄弟 | Xīn xiōngdì | New brothers | Recruits who joined after 1853; lower status than old brothers. |
| 湘军 | Xiāngjūn | Xiang Army | Hunan force associated with Zeng Guofan. |
| 淮军 | Huáijūn | Huai Army | Force associated with Li Hongzhang. |
| 常胜军 | Chángshèng jūn | Ever Victorious Army | Foreign-commanded Qing auxiliary force. |
| 团练 | Tuánliàn | Local militia | Gentry-organized local defense. |
| 乡勇 | Xiāngyǒng | Local braves | Locally raised irregular troops. |
| 绿营 | Lǜyíng | Green Standard Army | Qing regular force. |
| 八旗 | Bāqí | Eight Banners | Qing banner institution. |
| 粤匪 | Yuèfěi | Guangdong bandits | Hostile Qing term for Taiping. |
| 长毛 | Chángmáo | Long-hairs | Hostile term for Taiping followers. |
| 教匪 | Jiàofěi | Religious bandits | Qing hostile label. |
| 会匪 | Huìfěi | Society bandits | Qing hostile label. |
| 天京事变 | Tiānjīng shìbiàn | Tianjing Incident | 1856 internal purge and crisis. |
| 三河之战 | Sānhé zhī zhàn | Battle of Sanhe | Major Taiping victory in Anhui, 1858; Li Xiucheng and Chen Yucheng defeated a Xiang Army force. |
| 苏州杀降 | Sūzhōu shā xiáng | Suzhou surrender massacre | Mass execution of Taiping commanders who had surrendered Suzhou to Li Hongzhang, December 1863. |
| 江南大营 | Jiāngnán dàyíng | Jiangnan Great Camp | Qing siege camp near Tianjing. |
| 江北大营 | Jiāngběi dàyíng | Jiangbei Great Camp | Qing siege camp north of the Yangzi. |
| 安庆之战 | Ānqìng zhī zhàn | Battle of Anqing | Major middle Yangzi campaign. |
| 客家 | Kèjiā | Hakka | Ethnic group; many early Taiping leaders and followers were Hakka. |
| 本地 | Běndì | Punti / local people | Cantonese-speaking majority in Guangdong; term for non-Hakka locals. |
| 土客械斗 | Tǔ Kè xièdòu | Hakka-Punti armed conflict | Large-scale communal violence between Hakka and Punti communities, especially in Guangdong and Guangxi, which provided the recruitment base for the Taiping and their opponents. |
| 农民战争 | Nóngmín zhànzhēng | Peasant war | PRC historiographical term. |
| 反封建 | Fǎn fēngjiàn | Anti-feudal | Marxist interpretive term. |
| 政教合一 | Zhèng jiào hé yī | Union of politics and religion | Analytical term for religious state authority. |
